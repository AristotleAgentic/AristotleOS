Research paper9 pages6G / intermittent connectivity

A Governance Plane for AI-Native 6G Architectures Under Intermittent Connectivity

This paper frames intermittent connectivity as an accountability problem for AI-native 6G. Deep-edge and non-terrestrial network elements may need to act while disconnected from the operator domain, creating a gap between autonomous action and institutional oversight.

The proposed Governance Plane complements the data and management planes by carrying delegated authority, Policy as Code, runtime constraint enforcement, safety fallback modes, escalation logic, and cryptographic attestation for later synchronization.

Why it matters: it establishes the disconnection thesis: governance cannot depend on continuous cloud reachability. Authority and evidence have to travel with the edge.

Research paper5 pagesExecution boundary

Deterministic Governance Enforcement for Autonomous Infrastructure

This paper introduces the Petersen Governance Plane Architecture and the idea of Governance Invariant Compilation. It argues that model guardrails and runtime policy reasoning are not enough for infrastructure, because execution environments require deterministic safety constraints and institutional authority boundaries.

The central move is architectural: transform high-level governance artifacts into runtime invariants that can be checked at the commit point before infrastructure is touched.

Why it matters: it turns the Aristotle Agentic thesis into an enforcement model: authority is compiled into the path of action, not debated after the action escapes.

Book manuscript115 pagesGovernance infrastructure

The G-Plane Architecture: Governance Infrastructure for Autonomous Systems

This book-length manuscript develops the broader architecture behind Aristotle Agentic: governance infrastructure for autonomous systems operating in telecommunications, logistics, financial systems, robotics, distributed computing, and other infrastructure environments.

The motivating question is explicit: how do institutions maintain authority over machines that increasingly make operational decisions on their own? The book answers by treating governance as infrastructure, not institutional decoration.

Why it matters: it provides the philosophical and architectural backbone for the whole site: Aristotle Agentic as thought lab, AristotleOS as substrate, Training Hub as capacity building, and Montana AI-X as civic application.
